"Our goal is to connect the students' interests to relevant and meaningful learning experiences in the real world." - Clearview Mission Statement

Creating the Clearview STEM program, I focused on integrating student interests, connecting STEM to real-world experiences, and fostering engagement through hands-on activities. The curriculum aimed to be engaging,enjoyable, and technology-driven, introducing students to STEM opportunities. Unique to this program was the emphasis on student choice, allowing them to explore topics of personal interest, enhancing their ownership and investment in learning.

I feel the end product aligns with Clearview’s mission statement, and is a reflection of the school’s values.

Define

• Project Summary •

Problem: Design and create a STEM program for 8th-grade students (ages 11-12) in a school district which lacks a dedicated STEM program and basic tools / materials for a hands-on oriented curriculum. 
Role: Curriculum designer | Teacher
Duration:September 2021 - 2022 (curriculum submission) Currently improving and developing 
Team:Independent
Constraints:Upon entering the district, there were no supplies available (e.g., scissors, glue, microscopes, magnifying glasses, rulers, scales). The biggest challenge was to create hands-on and engaging lessons with virtually no materials to work with. Students were each in possession of their own chromebooks.

Prepare

• Research •

My first step was to gather data, and learn what students care about to devise a curriculum that draws their attention.
I conducted a survey among approximately 600 middle school students over the course of one year. The survey included questions about their favorite subjects in school and topics they were interested in learning about. However, due to privacy considerations and ethical guidelines, I am unable to share specific survey responses without parental permission.

Here is a sample of the survey questions.

Students expressed interest in a wide range of topics, including physics, sports, art, computer science, and life science. However, they often felt that their interests were not integrated into their learning experiences at school. Additionally, students expressed a desire to understand how these subjects could be relevant to their future aspirations.

Based on survey feedback, I acknowledged the wide spectrum of student interests, posing a challenge in unifying them into a cohesive curriculum. This realization inspired me to include exploratory lessons where students select topics of personal interest to delve into further. This approach empowers students to explore their passions while understanding their relevance to the STEM field. Now, I had a vision for the STEM program.

• The Goals •

  1. Implement Exploratory Lessons:Develop a curriculum that includes exploratory lessons, giving students the autonomy to choose their own learning paths.
  2. Connect Interests to STEM: Ensure that these exploratory lessons demonstrate the connection between students' personal interests and various fields within STEM.
  3. Enhance Engagement and Relevance: Foster a learning environment where students can see the real-world applications and future career opportunities related to their chosen topics.

An exploratory learning experience that engages and encourages students to recognizetheir inherent interests in the STEM field.

One example of a lesson I came up with based around this vision statement was Design Your Own Experiment.
In this lesson, students will engage in learning the scientific process, mastering key vocabulary terms, and conducting in-depth research on a topic of their choice (with teacher approval). Additionally, they will explore how their chosen topic connects to STEM through a dedicated research component of the project.

Reflect

• Challenges and Solutions •

Throughout this project, I encountered challenges due to limited materials and resources. To address this, I employed creative solutions such as leveraging Chromebooks and encouraging students to bring in their own supplies. Additionally, setting clear expectations with students was crucial. I communicated upfront that if students didn't bring their experiment supplies, alternatives like conducting material-free experiments, such as social experiments using questionnaires, would be explored.

• Learning & takeaways •

Although I'm pleased with the established curriculum, I felt the need to enhance it with practical skills to better prepare students for their future endeavors.

Based on student reflection surveys, coding, particularly website design, emerged as a recurring interest. While preserving the exploratory approach, I have begun to introduce HTML and CSS into my STEM class. This allows students to integrate their personal interests by creating websites on topics that intrigue them. Additionally, through teaching coding, I aim to instill and reinforce the problem-solving process, empowering students with valuable skills for tackling challenges in any field.
